Wenjie Hua is a scholar who has demonstrated outstanding abilities in the fields of language research and historical studies. On his academic journey, he has continuously explored and achieved fruitful results.

He has a rich educational background. He majored in Ancient Chinese Philology at Wuhan University, currently with a GPA of 3.89/4, ranking first in his class. During this period, he studied many core courses, laying a solid foundation for his academic research. He also once studied as an exchange student at the University of Notre Dame in the United States, where he achieved an excellent GPA of 4.0 and took courses such as Research Methods in Computer Programming, Law's Empire, and Text Analysis with Python, broadening his academic vision.

In terms of research and internship experiences, Wenjie Hua has also performed excellently. He has served as a research assistant in multiple institutions, participating in many research projects, including organizing Chinese - English bilingual code - switching experiments, geographical information sorting, and digitizing ancient books. In addition, he also worked as an editorial assistant at Science Press, participating in the editing, publishing, and promotion of works related to the history of mathematics and biotechnology.
